Google Allo

The Google Assistant was first introduced with Google’s release of the chat app Allo. The app came with a wide set of features that set it apart from a regular messaging app, but the most remarkable was the Assistant. You could chat with it directly in a separate conversation or summon it into any conversation by typing @google and a question. It would also appear on its own when it detected a need for information. For example, if someone mentions going out to eat, it might produce a small label that reads Restaurants Nearby which, when tapped, will prompt the AI to provide a list of restaurants and their related information.

Google also made sure to add personality to Allo’s new AI by bringing in Google Doodle head Ryan Germick and ex-Pixar animator Emma Coats, who made it their goal to turn the Assistant into a character. They implemented humorous responses and made its speech more casual. When asked about Siri, the Assistant would speak of her as an old friend, implying it had some past.

Allo’s Google Assistant displayed many of the features today’s assistant is know for; however, there were some drawbacks. Its reminders, for example, could not sync with a Google account or calendar. Moreover, the advertised third-party integration was missing: Before Allo’s release, the company demonstrated the ability to make a reservation at a restaurant using OpenTable within the chat app, but the feature did not make it to the final product. Still, the software was a major step towards the future of Google Assistant.
