Google Home

The Google Assistant gained independence and garnered fame at the launch of the Google Home. In fact, for many people, this was the first time ever hearing of the Assistant. Of course, Google Home did more for the Assistant than simply giving it a body. It is a smart speaker designed to be a hub for all of your smart devices. At its release, the Google Home gave the user voice control over a limited number of devices like Philips Hue bulbs and Nest products; however, within a few months, it had control of over 5,000 devices. To make life even easier, Google implemented Routines. Once a Routine is established, a set list of actions can be triggered at a certain time or with a single command.

The Google Home also works well with media as It can cast video to a TV using a Chromecast and play music and podcasts straight through its speakers. It supports popular services like YouTube, Netflix, Google Play, Spotify, and Pandora. However, its audio quality falls short of that of its main competitor, the Amazon Echo with the assistant Alexa.

While the feature was missing at the release, the Google Home soon allowed for up to six Google accounts to be registered to one speaker using Voice Recognition.
